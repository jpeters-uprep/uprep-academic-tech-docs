# Makers Tools

## Safety and PPE

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is available for use in our Makerspaces.
There is a safety sign on the wall that discusses when it's best to consider
using protection like safety glasses, ear protection, gloves, masks, and
closed-toe shoes. Please keep safety first!

Location: Middle School Makerspace, Stagecraft, and ULab Makerspace

## 3D Printers

Our fleet of Bambu Lab 3D printers is capable of printing detailed character
models, 4-color parts, high-strength battle bots, and real-world terrain models.
Check out our
[CAD Software](../../software-and-vr/fully-supported-software/autodesk) in the
software page to learn how to design your own models, or find something others
made on [printables.com](https://www.printables.com/) or
[makerworld.com](https://makerworld.com/en). When you're ready, you can send
your prints to the 3D printing queue
[here](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSfNhn1FX-1Na4lZKWIz_8Eb2L4V_ihyRNtRy7iT8chjfKU_dQ/viewform?usp=header).

If you'd like to use Bambu Studio yourself to slice and print your model, come
to the ULab makerspace during US community time or after school or reach out to
June (jpeters@universityprep.org) to find time in the Middle School Makerspace.
Steps to print yourself:

1. Upload your 3MF or STL file to the
   [shared folder](https://uprep-my.sharepoint.com/personal/puma_universityprep_org/_layouts/15/onedrive.aspx?id=%2Fpersonal%2Fpuma%5Funiversityprep%5Forg%2FDocuments%2FDocuments%2FPrints&ga=1).

2. Log on to the share computer in the makerspace using the puma@ account

3. In the file Explorer, open the "Prints" folder listed in Favorites on the
   left to find your file.

4. Open it in Bambu Studio. If you need help with Bambu Studio, talk to June.

Location: Middle School Makerspace and ULab Makerspace

## Laser Cutter

We have an Epilog Fusion Maker 24" laser cutter. It can engrave a range of
materials as well as cut detailed shapes. Laser safety is posted next to the
printer, and training is required before operating the machine. Reach out to
June (jpeters@universtyprep.org) to discuss learning the basics or building your
next project.

Location: ULab Makerspace

## Vinyl Cutter

Cutting vinyl is not safe on the laser cutter, but the vinyl cutter is both fast
and easy to use. Instead of heat, it cuts with a small automated blade. It's
ideal for cutting heat-transfer vinyl or sticky vinyl. When paired with the heat
press, it creates beautiful t-shirts, bags, and other products.

Location: ULab Makerspace

## Sublimation Printer

This works very similarly to a standard printer, but it prints on sublimation
paper instead of regular printer paper. After printing, you can use the heat
press to transfer the ink to a polyester shirt or bag!

Location: ULab Makerspace

## Heat Presses

Our heat presses make heat-transfer vinyl and sublimation prints permanent.
They're relatively simple to use - all you need to know are the temperature and
time for your specific application. There is a sign on the counter near the heat
presses with this information for common materials.

Location: ULab Makerspace

## Hand Tools

We have a wide variety of hand tools. Some of the more interesting include
digital and brass calipers, ratchets, rasps, and hack saws. We of course also
have screwdrivers, hammers, measuring tools, pliers, wrenches, etc.

Location: ULab Makerspace and Stagecraft

## Drones

[Our CoDrones](https://www.robolink.com/products/codrone-edu?srsltid=AfmBOorvAE4sYUyUQ_m37pgyzzk1iCNB6SM_ySjh4bYEHPa9NSBcJ_2I)
allow both manual flight via controller and programmed flight via programming.
We have both standard EDU and the Mini version. Students can learn to fly them,
program them, and work through obstacle courses.

Location: ULab Makerspace

## BirdBrain Circuits

[BirdBrain](https://www.birdbraintechnologies.com/) is a company founded through
Carnegie Mellon's CreateLab. We have Finch robots and Hummingbird kits, both of
which teach programming, circuits, and physical computing. They have input
sensors and output controls including lights, displays, and motion.

Location: MS Makerspace

## 3D Pens

3D pens are like a 3D printer that you control with your hand, like a regular
pen. You can design relatively flat things like snowflakes, or very complex tall
things like a model of the Eiffel Tower! These require good ventilation, so be
sure to use them in a room where the air is moving consistently.

Location: ULab Makerspace

## CNC

Our CNC, or computer controlled router, allows cutting and carving of wood and
plastic.
